Gopher & Chinchilla

Both trained at the same compute budget: C = 5.76×10²³ FLOPs. They sit far to the right of the experimental data, in the extrapolation regime of all scaling laws.

Gopher (280B) N = 280B · D = 300B tokens L̂ ≈ 1.993 (undertrained) Kaplan-era recipe: big model, few tokens
Chinchilla (70B) N = 70B · D = 1.4T tokens L̂ ≈ 1.937 (compute-optimal) 4× smaller, 4× more data than Gopher

The Approach 1 frontier predicts N = 69.3B at this compute — matching Chinchilla almost exactly. The Kaplan curve predicts N = 491B — 4× over-sized relative to compute-optimal.

Fig 4 right 114 pts · 9 slices

Extracted from Figure 4 (right) of Hoffmann et al. 2022 via PDF vector-graphics parsing. 9 groups each hold C = constant; D = C/6N adjusts. The scatter reveals the loss sweet spot for each compute budget.

The raw frontier picks the discrete minimum per slice — jagged due to shared model-size grids. The smooth frontier fits N∝C⁰·⁴⁷⁵ through those 9 minima.

Fig 4 left — bias-fixed L̂(N,D)
L̂(N,D) = E + A/Nα + B/Dβ
E=1.8172 · A=482.01 · B=2085.43 · α=0.3478 · β=0.3658

Smooth dashed curves at the same 9 FLOPs budgets as the Fig 4 right scatter, using the Besiroglu et al. 2024 bias-corrected coefficients (arXiv:2404.10102). The original Hoffmann et al. 2022 fit (E=1.69, A=406.4, B=410.7, α=0.34, β=0.28) was shown to be biased: on the 245 Epoch AI points the mean |ΔL| drops from 0.061 → 0.027 under the fix, and the compute-optimal exponent moves from N∝C⁰·⁴⁵² (disagreeing with Approaches 1 & 2) to N∝C⁰·⁵¹³ (consistent).

The three terms capture: irreducible text entropy (E), model capacity error (A/Nα), and data sub-optimality (B/Dβ).

Chinchilla Approaches 1–3

Approach 1 (N∝C⁰·⁵⁰) — Fix model sizes, vary tokens; extract envelope.

Nopt = 0.224·(C/6)0.50

Approach 2 (N∝C⁰·⁴⁹) — Fit parabola to each isoFLOP curve minimum.

Nopt = 0.350·(C/6)0.49

Approach 3 (N∝C⁰·⁴⁵) — Minimise L̂ analytically. Most data-centric.

Nopt = 1.65·(C/6)0.452

All three agree that at Gopher's compute the optimal N ≈ 40–70B (vs Gopher's 280B).

Prior Scaling Laws

Kaplan et al. (2020) (N∝C⁰·⁷³) — Predicts N≈491B at Gopher budget. The steep exponent meant the field was building ever-larger undertrained models.

Henighan et al. (2020) (N∝C⁰·⁷³) — Validates Kaplan for language; different coefficient gives N≈154B at Gopher budget.
